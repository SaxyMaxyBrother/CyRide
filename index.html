What’s broken on weekends?

On paper, CyRide runs seven days a week. In reality, weekends feel like a different system. Here’s the core problem in three simple pieces.

Waits are too long

30–45+ minutes between buses on key routes.

Miss one bus on a weekend and you might lose your study session, shift, or game. Shorter, predictable waits would make transit usable, not just “available.”

Coverage shrinks

Some apartments & jobs lose direct service.

Fewer weekend routes mean students walk long distances or pay for rideshares to reach places they can easily reach by bus during the week.

Safety has gaps

SafeRide & late-night options aren’t enough.

Late-night coverage is limited, and “on-demand” options can be slow or full. Reliable fixed-route service to 11 PM would keep more Cyclones out of risky situations.

Students already ride

Millions of trips prove we depend on CyRide.

Weekend improvements aren’t a luxury; they match how ISU students, staff, and families actually move around Ames.

How weekend service actually feels

These short scenarios are based on real ISU student experiences. If any of them sound familiar, you already know why weekend CyRide needs to improve.

Group project that never meets

A group schedules a Sunday work session at Frederiksen. One member from South Duff misses the bus and the next one isn’t for 40 minutes. The group gives up and meets on Zoom instead.

Closing shift on the line

A student working a weekend closing shift in Campustown plans to take CyRide home. The last bus leaves just before they clock out, so they either leave early, walk late at night, or pay for a rideshare.

Groceries become a math problem

Two roommates time a Saturday grocery run around a 35-minute bus gap. When the checkout line runs long, they have to sprint with bags to avoid being stuck waiting outside with frozen food.

Late study session, early cutoff

A student studies at the library until 9:30 PM on Sunday. Their bus home runs only once an hour, and SafeRide is backed up. They leave early instead of finishing the assignment.
