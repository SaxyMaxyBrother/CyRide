What would ‚Äúbetter‚Äù weekend CyRide look like?

These proposals build on routes CyRide already operates and extend them to match how students actually use Ames on Saturdays and Sundays.

1. Core campus routes on weekends

Bring at least one campus-focused route (like #21 Cardinal or #23 Orange) into regular weekend service to connect major residence halls, campus hubs, and Campustown.

This would give car-free students dependable access to libraries, the Memorial Union, rec facilities, and events without relying on friends for rides.

2. Stronger weekend frequency on key lines

Increase the frequency of core routes that already run weekends (#1 Red, #2 Green, #3 Blue). Shortening waits from, for example, 60 minutes to 30 minutes would make buses a realistic option for errands, jobs, and social plans.

Even modest changes to weekend timetables can dramatically boost ridership and reduce risky late-night driving.

3. Protect Moonlight Express & extend breaks

Moonlight Express currently offers free rides anywhere in Ames on Friday and Saturday nights from 10:30 p.m. to 2:30 a.m. when ISU is in session.

Expanding this model to cover parts of summer and more break periods would keep students safer year-round, especially those working late shifts or living off campus.
